Guided Imagery

According to cognitive behavioral theory, emotions are formed from thoughts.

So, if intrusive thoughts can be regulated, the feelings that result from them are more manageable.

To get you into a deeper state of relaxation, the imagery makes use of all five senses.

Go back
Instruction

Step 1: Sit or lie down comfortably. Try to avoid any distractions.

Step 2: If you want, you can recall one of your memories, when you were feeling peaceful and relaxed. If you prefer, you can create one through imagination! Let it run wild and free.

Now, focus on what you can see. Is it a deep, blue color of the water or green, calming forest?

What do you hear? Is it waves crashing along the shore or chirping birds?

What do you smell? Maybe is it fruity aromas from sunscreen you just used, or the fresh scent of flowers?

What do you taste? Is it the salty sea air, or a freshly picked berry?

What do you feel? Warmth of the sun, or a summer drizzle falling on your skin?

Step 3: Keep the visualization going for as long as you can while concentrating on taking calm, deep breaths.
